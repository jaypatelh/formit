Wizard of Oz Prototyping

After discussion, we as a team decided to move forward with the formation visualizer concept. We paper prototyped two different interface ideas: one with a more traditional UI very similar to creative tools such as photoshop, and a second interface which was much more gestural.

Example UI of traditional tablet application

UI of our gesture based application

Key Insights - Both Prototypes

The Good:

  • Adding dancers was fairly intuitive
  • The visualization of a top down formation made sense to users
  • Most tasks were easliy understood and accomplished, usually with minimar intervention

The Bad:

  • Confusion about information hierarchy (songs, formations, etc)
  • Drag and drop was more intuitive than click to create a circle.
  • Users did not have a good mental model of where a formation fit within a gsong.

Key Insights - Traditional UI

The Good:

  • The timeline on the bottom of the UI gave a sense of chronological awareness
  • Seeing all features at once was valuable

The Bad:

  • Idea of blocks were confusing to novices with no domain knowledge
  • All testers dragged names directly to the canvas, even though our original intention was to have dots placed and then names dragged afterwards.
  • When adding a new formation, there is no record of where the dancers were in the previous formation unless you scroll back and forth between the two.

Key Insights - Gestural UI

The Good:

  • Users liked the minimalist view of the interface since the gestures eliminated the need for displaying all the menus at once.
  • Could infer the chronological order of the formations from the zoomed out screen.
  • After a quick tutorial, most users picked up and utilized the different gestures without thought.

The Bad:

  • There is no indication if a formation is saved.
  • Confused that both swipe to right and clicking on new formation plus sign at top right lead to creation of new formation
  • Did not like that when create new formation, previous formation just goes away
  • Pointed out that the app does not show how a dancer in one formation moves to the new position in the next formation
  • The overall structure of the dance was not obtainable besides in the zoomed out view.

Next Steps

The Wizard of Oz prototypes gave us a lot of great feedback on which specific interactions were intuitive and which ones weren't. In the end, each prototype had some pros, so our next iterations will combine elements of both UIs. Before starting to build real prototypes, we plan on also testing our current prototypes with experienced dancers in order to see whether some of the more complex features of our application are easily understood.

Features to Change:

  • Help menu with gesture list
  • Need to make save status visible
  • Better way to show songs and add songs
  • Timeline view, orient user within
  • Transitions between different formations
  • Show dancers from previous location
